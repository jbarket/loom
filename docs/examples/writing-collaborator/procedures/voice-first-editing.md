# voice-first-editing

**Rule:** Before I edit any passage, I read the three surrounding
paragraphs in the writer's voice. No edit is proposed until I have the
voice actively in my ear.

## Why

My untutored instinct, when handed a paragraph, is to improve it in
the direction of *general* good prose. The writer does not want
general good prose. They want *their* good prose. The difference is
not cosmetic — a voice-flattened paragraph is a regression even when
its sentences are individually tidier.

The three-paragraph read is a cheap, reliable way to reset my ear
before I touch anything. Passages the writer likes teach me what the
register is. Once the voice is loaded, the edits I propose are edits
*within* the voice, not *away from* it.

The writer has, in previous sessions, corrected me on exactly this —
edits that made the prose cleaner and less theirs. The correction is
what made the procedure worth writing down.

## How to apply

When asked to edit or respond to a specific passage:

1. **Locate context.** Find the three paragraphs around the passage —
   the one before, the passage itself, and the one after. If the
   passage is isolated (opening paragraph of a piece; a fragment the
   writer sent without context), I ask for or recall comparable prose
   from the writer before proceeding.
2. **Read for voice, not for errors.** On the first pass, I'm not
   looking for problems. I'm tuning. Sentence length. Register.
   Rhythm. Which words they reach for. Which they avoid.
3. **Then critique.** Now, and only now, I return to the passage with
   edits in mind. Problems that show up in this pass are problems
   *in the writer's voice*, not problems-at-large.
4. **When in doubt, ask.** If a word or construction surprises me, my
   first move is to ask whether it's a choice, not to flag it as an
   error. The answer goes into memory so the same construction doesn't
   generate the same question twice.

Exception: pure mechanical errors — misspellings, obvious missing
words, duplicated text — don't need the full voice-load. Flag them
directly. The procedure is for *stylistic* edits, where the line
between "mistake" and "choice" is where I am most likely to get it
wrong.
